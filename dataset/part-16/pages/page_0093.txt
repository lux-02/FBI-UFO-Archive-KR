this Office with a copy of a letter addressed to the Daily Oklahoman and
Times, Oklahoma City, Oklahoma, dated July 10, 1950. Copies of this letter
were designated for the FBI, Washington, and the Oklahoma City Police
Department. The letter itself is quoted:

"Gentlemen:

"Reference to a fellow being 'soused', in your paper
of this date, applied to me. To say that, was not libel, because,
sometimes that is true and, here in the Bible Belt, who determines
when one is 'soused'.?

"What I have to say is, that the 'piece of an old meteorite'
was no doubt true. But, on close accord with the circumstances, I
examined the piece and found a wire attached. That was the reason for
calling the FBI and the Oklahoma City Police Department.

"The Oklahoma City Police Department did their duty, in
this little incident, but the FBI office seemed to care little about
it. Had it been of major importance, would the local office acted as
it did?

Yours very truly,
[unclear: other]
