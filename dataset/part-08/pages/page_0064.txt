REPORT: Unidentified Flying Objects Observed in Oregon 27 May 1949:

On Friday, 27 May 1949, source was flying his own SNJ-type
aircraft from Red Bluff, California to Burns, Oregon, a distance of 300
miles. He left Red Bluff at 1030 P.S.T. and arrived at Burns at 1418 P.S.T.,
time 3 hours 48 minutes. Source landed at Burns after circling the
airport for 10 minutes. While on the ground, source's aircraft was serviced
with 170 gallons of gasoline which he had purchased at Red Bluff.
During this flight he made 212 MPH ground speed; and
estimates he had a tail wind of 15 to 18 MPH.

At 1423 Pacific Standard Time, Friday, 27 May 1949, source of
the SNJ-type aircraft observed an object at a considerable distance
a few points to the southwest. He continued to watch the course taken

The objects that source saw are described by him as follows:
Size of each object: Considerably smaller than a fighter plane.
probably less than 20' in length. All of the separate objects appeared to be
the same in size.
Shape of objects: There was no break in the outline. Source
certain he would have recognized conventional aircraft. They had a solid
configuration, and no great thickness. They were elongated oval, perhaps
five times as long as wide, and source could not estimate their thickness;
but he observed the objects from an angle from above and could not estimate
whether they could possibly have been egg-shaped, or approximately
concavely have been perfectly oval. The objects seemed definitely to be
objects - there was nothing attached about them.
Speed of objects: Source is confident they were traveling through
the air at 212 MPH. Source had a tail wind of 15 to 18 MPH, and therefore
had been doing over 230 MPH air speed. The
objects were traveling south/southeast, opposite to source's course.
Calculating the speed of the objects, several different ways from the
head, the speed of the objects varies between 190 to 260 MPH. Source
concluded at the time of observation that he could not possibly have
around and caught up with the objects. They were definitely traveling
slower than jet planes. No trail of smoke or
exhaust was observed. The speed of the objects appeared to be steady.
